Why Music Matters

We don't teach instruments.
We grow people.

A child who learns music learns something deeper: how to try, how to fail kindly, how to keep going, and how to stand in a room and be fully themselves. That stays long after the last note.

Confidence

Every lesson ends in a small win. Those wins compound into a child who believes they can learn hard things.

Creativity

We teach improvisation and writing early — so students don't just play music, they make it their own.

Growth

Practice teaches patience and discipline that quietly shows up in school, sport, and life.

Expression

Music gives feelings a language. For many students, it becomes the place they're most themselves.

Good questions

Everything you're wondering

Where do you travel for in-home lessons?

We teach in-home across Sacramento, Roseville, Rocklin, Folsom, Granite Bay, Loomis, Lincoln, Fair Oaks, Citrus Heights, and Elk Grove. Outside that radius, online lessons are available worldwide with the same teachers.

How much do lessons cost?

Private lessons are $60 for a 45-minute lesson or $40 for a 30-minute lesson — and that includes us coming to your home. No registration fees, no hidden pricing, no long-term contracts. Online and studio lessons are the same rate.

How does scheduling work?

You pick a recurring weekly time that fits your family — mornings, after school, evenings, or weekends. Need to move a lesson? Just let your teacher know in advance and we'll find another slot.

Do you really teach online?

Yes. Our online lessons are structured, camera-ready, and just as personal as in-home — ideal for busy weeks, travel, or families outside our driving radius.

Do you work with charter & homeschool families?

Absolutely — charter and homeschool families are a big part of MuzHouse. We're familiar with charter enrichment funding and can provide the documentation and invoicing your program needs.

What does a typical lesson look like?

Most students take a weekly 30, 45, or 60-minute lesson. Each is one-on-one and built around your goals — technique, repertoire, theory, and creativity — with regular recitals and recording opportunities.
